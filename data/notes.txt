A three-stage pipeline turns raw notes into a graph
- parse_notes.py splits notes.txt on --- delimiters
- embed_notes.py encodes each note as a vector
- build_graph.py clusters and links by similarity
- run_pipeline.py runs all three steps in order

---

Notes are written as plain text blocks
- the first line becomes the node label
- dashed lines become the detail points
- a blank --- separator divides each note
- no database, just one flat text file

---

Sentence-transformers embeds each note
- all-MiniLM-L6-v2 produces 384-dim vectors
- it runs locally with no API call
- the title and details embed together
- embeddings.npy caches the matrix

---

Cosine similarity reveals hidden connections
- every note is compared to every other
- the k nearest neighbors become edges
- edge weight is the similarity score
- semantically close notes link up

---

K-means groups the notes into clusters
- six clusters partition the idea space
- each cluster gets an accent color
- cluster size hints at idea density
- clustering is recomputed on every run

---

A Flask server feeds the viewer
- server.py exposes /api/graph as JSON
- nodes and links load from the data files
- the static folder serves the single-page app
- it runs on port 5001 to dodge macOS AirPlay

---

The whole front-end is one HTML file
- 3d-force-graph wraps Three.js for the layout
- everything loads from a CDN with no build step
- MediaPipe Hands reads the webcam input
- a few hundred lines drive the entire scene

---

Force-directed layout finds equilibrium
- repulsion pushes the nodes apart
- edges pull related notes together
- the simulation settles then freezes
- a pinned hub anchors the center

---

A central hub ties the tree together
- the Second Brain node sits at the origin
- a spoke connects it to every note
- it stays fixed while the notes drift
- it gives the graph a readable center

---

Nodes glow with additive sprites
- a radial gradient texture fakes bloom
- each halo is tinted by its cluster color
- additive blending lights up the dark scene
- a slow pulse makes the glow breathe

---

A starfield adds depth behind the graph
- 1800 points fill a deep spherical shell
- slow rotation creates parallax
- a vignette darkens the screen edges
- the background sells a sense of space

---

Titles reveal by proximity
- only nearby notes show their label
- distant labels stay hidden to cut clutter
- the nearest band fades in as you approach
- the hub label always stays visible

---

Hand tracking replaces the mouse
- MediaPipe returns 21 landmarks per hand
- it runs in the browser over WebGL
- getUserMedia grabs the webcam directly
- the index fingertip becomes the cursor

---

Pointing steers the camera like a joystick
- fingertip position sets a rotation velocity
- a dead-zone stops a steady point from drifting
- left and right swing the view around
- only an extended index finger engages it

---

Hover-to-select feels more fluid than clicking
- the node nearest the fingertip highlights
- a short dwell commits the selection
- no pinch or click is required
- the choice follows your hand continuously

---

Pinch zooms into a node
- the camera flies in to frame the node
- releasing eases back to the overview
- moving while pinched orbits around it
- the grabbed node stays selected throughout

---

Details bloom into subnodes on pinch
- each bullet becomes a dot around the parent
- golden-angle spacing spreads them evenly
- connector lines tie them to the node
- tiny orange dots stream along the lines

---

Selecting a node lights up its neighbors
- connected edges brighten to white
- the rest of the graph dims away
- neighbor glows grow brighter
- extra particles flow along the hot links

---

The camera lives in spherical coordinates
- theta and phi set the orbit angle
- the scene slowly spins when idle
- a focus factor eases the zoom in and out
- the home distance frames the whole tree

---

Typography sets the tech mood
- Orbitron labels the hub and headers
- Share Tech Mono carries the note text
- labels render to canvas sprite textures
- the fonts load before the scene draws

---

Cross-version Three.js powers the custom objects
- the graph library bundles its own Three.js
- a separate build exposes a global THREE
- it draws the glow, lines, and text sprites
- basic meshes render across the versions

---

A HUD shows the graph at a glance
- live counts of notes, links, and clusters
- a color-coded cluster legend
- an intro title fades into the scene
- it all shares the same tech typography
